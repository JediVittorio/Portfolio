Worked with a 10k+ row sales dataset in Excel, used Power Query to clean and reshape the data, then built pivot tables and slicers to summarize performance by time, region, and product category.

Tooling: Excel, Power Query, Pivot Tables Data Size: 10,000+ rows (CSV) Focus: Clean → Model → Summarize

Project Overview

Goal

Demonstrate comfort working with “large‑ish” operational data in Excel, going beyond simple filters: import and clean a 10k+ row dataset, model it in a way that’s pivot‑friendly, and distill thousands of rows into a handful of business‑ready insights.

  • Built multiple pivot tables with slicers for interactive analysis.

Dataset (10k+ Row CSV)

The core data represents item‑level sales transactions that can be swapped for any open data CSV with similar fields (e.g., retail, orders, or revenue datasets).

Column

Power Query / Cleaning Steps

  • Imported the CSV into Excel using Power Query (“Get & Transform”).
  • Removed duplicate rows and obvious blanks for key fields.
  • Standardized region and product category labels (fixed typos/casing).
  • Split composite columns (e.g., “Region – Country”) into separate fields.
  • Derived additional columns like Month, Quarter, and Year from Order_Date.
  • Calculated Revenue in Power Query so pivot tables reference a clean field.

Pivot Tables & Slicers

Pivot by Time

Built pivot tables that summarize the dataset by Month and Quarter, using the derived date fields from Power Query.

  • Monthly Revenue Pivot – Rows: Month / Year; Values: sum of Revenue; Columns: Region for quick side‑by‑side comparison.
  • Quarterly Volume Pivot – Rows: Quarter; Values: sum of Units, sum of Revenue; Filters: Channel, Product_Category.

Pivot by Category

Focused on where sales are concentrated and which categories are driving growth.

  • Region x Product Category – Rows: Region; Columns: Product_Category; Values: Revenue and Units.
  • Channel Performance – Rows: Channel; Values: Revenue, Average Discount; Filters: Region, Quarter.

Slicers / Interactivity

Added slicers so stakeholders can answer “what if” questions without touching formulas.

  • Slicers for Region, Product_Category, Channel, and Year.
  • Timeline slicer for Order_Date to quickly move between months and quarters.
  • Pivots are connected to the same slicers, so all views update together.

Insights & What It Proves

Sample Insights (Business‑Level)

  • Quarter‑over‑quarter growth is concentrated in two regions. The West and South regions grew revenue ~18–22% over the last two quarters, while other regions were relatively flat, suggesting opportunity to replicate what’s working there.
  • Discount‑heavy channels don’t always drive better volume. Partner sales showed the highest average discount, but only modest uplift in units, indicating room to tighten discounting without sacrificing volume.
  • A small number of product categories carry most of the revenue. Across 10k+ rows, three categories consistently account for >70% of revenue, helping leadership prioritize inventory, marketing, and roadmap discussions.
