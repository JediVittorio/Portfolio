1. Problem Statement (Plain English)

Over the last several months, the customer support center has experienced inconsistent wait times, higher call volumes in certain queues, and mixed customer feedback. Leadership wants to understand:

  • When and where call demand is highest.
  • Which queues and call reasons are driving longer handle times and wait times.
  • How resolution rates and CSAT scores are trending over time.

The goal of this analysis is to provide a clear picture of current performance and outline practical steps to improve efficiency and customer experience.

2. Data & Approach

The report is based on a synthetic call‑level dataset (see Project 2) with columns such as Call_ID, Date, Customer_State, Queue, Agent_ID, Call_Reason, Handle_Time, Wait_Time, Resolution, and CSAT_Score.

  • Data prepared in Excel/SQLite and summarized using SQL.
  • KPIs calculated by day, week, month, and by queue / call reason.
  • Dashboard built in Power BI to visualize trends and anomalies.

This report highlights key charts from that dashboard and describes what they mean in everyday language.

4. Findings (Trends, Patterns, Anomalies)
  • Queue B’s calls are noticeably more complex. Its average handle time is roughly 35% higher than other queues, even after controlling for call volume.
  • Call volume spikes around billing cycle dates. On those days, both total calls and wait times increase, particularly for customers in certain states.
  • High wait‑time days align with specific events. Outages and marketing campaigns correlate with days where average wait time is significantly above normal.
  • Better resolution rates track with higher CSAT. Queues that solve more issues on the first contact also show stronger satisfaction scores.
5. Recommendations (If I were the ops manager, I would…)
  • Target Queue B for process review and training. Shadow agents, map the most common call reasons, and create focused scripts or knowledge‑base content to reduce handle time without rushing customers.
  • Stabilize billing‑related call spikes. Coordinate with Billing and Communications to send clearer reminders and FAQs ahead of billing dates, especially to states that show the biggest surges.
  • Plan staffing around predictable high‑volume windows. Use the daily call trend chart to schedule extra coverage on known peak days, and reduce coverage where demand is consistently low.
  • Use resolution rate + CSAT as core quality metrics. Build these into agent scorecards and coaching, rewarding behaviors that close issues on the first contact while maintaining high CSAT.
6. Next Steps & Communication Plan

The final section of the PDF outlines how these findings will be shared across teams:

  • Short slide‑style summary for leadership with top 3 metrics.
  • Targeted follow‑up with queue supervisors to review queue‑level charts.
  • Regular monthly refresh of the dashboard and report to track progress.

This ensures the analysis doesn’t live only in a dashboard, but becomes part of an ongoing conversation between operations, training, IT, and marketing.
